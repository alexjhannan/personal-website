I started teaching myself how to code in August of 2015. I had prior experience with some C, C++, and SQL, but learning JavaScript was a new endeavor - and a career goal.

These Codepens are examples of some of my earliest projects. FreeCodeCamp's curriculum served as a series of signposts for the early days.

These simple projects only include front-end work. Once I became familiar with NodeJS, I stepped into creating simple back-ends. For some examples of full-stack projects, see my GitHub page, below.

Without coding, my career was leading me down the path of an educator. A good teacher fully understands his or her students by fully understanding his or her self. My efforts to better understand my students pushed me straight into a line of inquiry that I continue to digest, write, think, and talk about today.

I call this paradigm of information 'Kaleidoscope' - for goofy reasons - and I invite the curious to browse on. Whether any of this is useful is beyond me, though my enjoyment of the process is quite enough.
